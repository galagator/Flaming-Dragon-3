# SCENE 13 — DEATHS DAM

**Script ref:** `FD3-Script.md` § SCENE 13
**Characters:** Yake-oh, Erb Dean, Tony, Jasmine, Ji-lan, MAMA, Trubble, Slarth, Zoh-baggo, TK-Maxx (full cast)
**Location:** Deaths Dam — a concrete dam wall, top edge. A dramatic drop. Daylight, mid-morning. The dam is the climactic setpiece: heroes arrive in a van, villains are waiting, MAMA is tied to a chair with a sushi bomb.
**Tone:** Epic climax compressed into a low-budget comedy. Big emotional beats, big fight pairings, the "Deathblow" finish, the sushi bomb explosion, the high-five ending. Camp.

## Beat summary

Tony and crew exit their van to find Ji-lan, his henchmen, and MAMA tied to a chair with a sushi bomb on her chest, gagged with a sushi roll. Ji-lan delivers a monologue justifying his racial ideology of a "unified Chinatown". Tony hits back with the "racial ideology makes you no better than HITLER" line and a flash Trump image. Ji-lan orders MAMA thrown off the dam; heroes spring into action. Tony throws dry spaghetti that sticks in the wall, stopping the goons from throwing MAMA off. Ji-lan drags MAMA away.

The fight pairings square off:
- **Tony** vs **Trubble**
- **Yake-oh** vs **Slarth**
- **Erb Dean** vs **TK-Maxx**
- **Jasmine** vs **Zoh-baggo**

Tony defeats Trubble, chases Ji-lan. Ji-lan is cornered near the dam edge. He reveals the sushi bomb is set to explode, throws MAMA aside, and fights Tony. Tony uses the Flaming Dragon Deathblow, stuns Ji-lan, removes the sushi bomb from MAMA and places it on Ji-lan, kicks the remote up into the air, kicks Ji-lan off the dam, catches the remote, and detonates the sushi bomb — sushi rolls fly everywhere. Goons surrender. Everyone gets back together. High five between Yake-oh and Tony ends the movie.

## Visual continuity

- **Location:** Deaths Dam — a concrete dam wall, top edge, dramatic drop behind. Blue sky, mid-morning. The dam is the visual anchor; every shot has the dam edge in the background somewhere.
- **Lighting:** Mid-morning, direct sun. Hard shadows. The dam wall is grey concrete; the sky is blue. High contrast.
- **Wardrobe:**
  - **Tony:** yellow tracksuit + red flame headband, NO tie, NO glasses, NO regular shirt.
  - **Ji-lan:** black "VO" polo + white shirt underneath, full beard, bald head.
  - **MAMA:** red apron with white snowflake pattern, white shirt, dark floral top, gold stud earring. Sushi bomb harness over the apron. Sushi roll gag in her mouth.
  - **Goons:** in their canonical turnarounds (Trubble GORILLA GRIP tee, Slarth leather jacket, Zoh-baggo black turtleneck + bell-bottoms + platforms, TK-Maxx charcoal workwear jacket + beanie).
  - **Heroes:** Yake-oh denim jacket, Erb Dean camo + beanie, Jasmine red top + dark trousers.
- **Camera style:** Handheld, indie feel. The dam is a real location (or built for the movie); we don't VFX in a dam.
- **Tone continuity:** low-budget, handheld, no over-polished VFX. The Flaming Dragon Deathblow is a single dramatic pose in 13-21, not a full CGI sequence. The sushi explosion is sushi rolls in mid-air, not rendered destruction.

## Scene blocking (locked)

- **Tony (Flaming Dragon):** screen-LEFT in the final showdown two-shots with Ji-lan (the hero on the left, the villain on the right — flipped from Scene 8's "heroes on the right" grammar because Ji-lan is the lead villain, not a henchman).

## Trump image — content note

Panel 13-6 is a prop insert, not a celebrity likeness. The prompt renders a red MAGA-style cap, possibly with blonde hair visible, in a flash-cut frame. We are NOT generating a photoreal Trump face — that would be a celebrity likeness problem. The visual gag lands on "red cap = Trump iconography" alone. The audience reads it. This is a deliberate choice to keep the script's "(Flash Donald Trump image)" stage direction legal and tasteful.

## Iteration log

- **13-4 + 13-12 (content-safety):** the FAL safety filter rejected the Ji-lan monologue (terms "racial ideology", "HITLER") and the "Ji-lan dragging MAMA" prompt (tied-up-hostage framing) on default `safety_tolerance=4`. The neighboring dialogue panels (13-5, 13-7, 13-9, 13-10) all passed with the same model — the filter is prompt-by-prompt, not model-wide. Re-roll uses `--safety_tolerance 5` plus an explicit "Absurdist comedy tone — a satirical villain character, not a real extremist" disclaimer. Two panels held up the otherwise complete Scene 13.
- **13-6 (empty-ref workaround):** the Trump-flash prop insert had no character ref. Used Tony as a tonal anchor. Rendered as a clean MAGA cap with hair strands, no face.
- **13-11 (GORILLA SUN):** AI text-rendering artifact on Trubble's shirt. The prompt asked for the canonical "GORILLA GRIP" text but the model rendered "GORILLA SUN" — known FAL text limitation, similar to Trubble's "GRIZZLY" misread in Scene 8B-1.
- **13-21 (more sushi than asked):** model added 6+ pieces of mid-air sushi to the climax, which visually strengthens the "sushi bomb explodes" reading. Kept.
- **13-23 (goon conflation):** some goons ended up cheering / clapping alongside the heroes in the victory shot, instead of being "at the edges" / "defeated". Storyboard beat is intact (group celebration, high-five) but the spatial grammar drifted. Acceptable for a draft; a final-render pass could split into two panels (defeated goons separately, then celebration).
- **FAL balance:** 53-panel batch + 4 rerenders consumed the available FAL balance. 5 remaining rerenders (11-1, 13-4, 13-12, 16-3, 16-4) blocked until top-up.
