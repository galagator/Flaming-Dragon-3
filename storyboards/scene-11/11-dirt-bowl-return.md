# SCENE 11 — BACK AT THE DIRT BOWL

**Script ref:** `FD3-Script.md` § SCENE 11
**Characters:** Yake-oh, Erb Dean, Tony, Jasmine
**Location:** Same Dirt Bowl from Scene 9 — a dirt lot with a circle of cars, party crowd, sparring in the middle. Late afternoon / evening light. 80s party energy continues.
**Tone:** Hero-return celebration. Triumphant entry, then earnest rallying, then a low-brow sex gag. Comedy carries the day.

## Beat summary

Tony walks back into the Dirt Bowl wearing the Flaming Dragon headband + new outfit. The party crowd notices; Yake-oh and Erb Dean are stunned. Tony runs into the sparring circle and — in sped-up footage — takes down a string of sparrers while his friends watch. He then asks the crew to come with him to Deaths Dam tomorrow. They agree. Jasmine, watching from a car bonnet, is visibly turned on. Tony crosses to her with a confident bulge. They retire to a tent; a silhouette outline is implied on canvas; camera pans away to black to the sound of love.

## Visual continuity

- **Location:** The same Dirt Bowl lot from Scene 9 — circle of cars, party crowd, sparring area in the middle. We don't have storyboard panels for Scene 9 yet; this scene is the visual reference for that beat's look.
- **Lighting:** Late afternoon → evening. Car headlights are the dominant practical. Warm tungsten from inside cars and the tent. Low-budget indie — the headlights can clip / flare slightly, that's the look.
- **Wardrobe:**
  - **Tony:** yellow tracksuit + red flame headband (per Scene 10's 10-14 / 10-15 finals). No tie, no glasses, no regular shirt.
  - **Yake-oh / Erb Dean / Jasmine:** their canonical turnaround outfits (denim jacket / camo + beanie / red top + dark trousers).
- **Camera style:** Handheld, indie feel. The montage beats (11-7/8/9) read as "sped up" through silhouette + varied moves, not through blur.
- **Tone continuity with Scene 8:** low-budget, handheld, no over-polished VFX. The headband and tracksuit carry the "Flaming Dragon" status, the camera doesn't need pyrotechnics.
